Molecular evolution research at Visva-Bharati

Sinha Molecular Evolution Group

We study how proteins, genes, and molecular systems adapt through evolutionary time, combining phylogenetic inference, structural interpretation, and computational bioinformatics.

Selected Publications ORCID Profile

Research Identity

Molecular Evolution with Model-aware Computation

The group asks how adaptive molecular change can be detected, interpreted, and connected to biological function. Current work includes episodic positive selection, branch-site evolutionary models, protein stability, host-parasite interfaces, and learned scoring functions for sequence alignment.

Research

Core Programs

Our research integrates evolutionary biology, bioinformatics, and structural reasoning to study molecular adaptation across scales.

01

Episodic Positive Selection

Detecting lineage-specific bursts of adaptive evolution with branch-site models, simulation, and careful identifiability checks.

02

Protein Evolution and Function

Connecting substitutions to protein stability, interfaces, and predicted functional consequences in immune and signaling systems.

03

Bioinformatics Methods

Developing computational tools for sequence alignment, evolutionary inference, and likelihood-free analysis of molecular sequence data.
